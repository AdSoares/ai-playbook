# Reference — Pedagogy

> Load this when writing lessons, exercises, or flashcards. These are the learning methods every
> capsule must apply, plus Bloom's taxonomy for objectives and assessments.

The capsule must be built on recognized learning science. Content must never be passive text.

## 1. Active recall (retrieval practice)
Make the learner pull information from memory.
- Questions at the end of every section.
- Closed-book exercises.
- Flashcards.
- Progressive assessments.
- Ask the learner to explain a concept in their own words.

## 2. Distributed practice (spacing)
Even inside a ≤10h capsule, spread review and practice across the study.
- Quick reviews between lessons.
- Repeated questions with variation.
- Re-surface fundamental concepts in later blocks.
- Post-study review recommendations.

## 3. Self-explanation
Ask the learner to explain concepts in their own language.
- "Explain this concept as if teaching a layperson."
- "Give a real example."
- "Say when this concept does NOT apply."
- "Compare this concept with a similar one."

## 4. Worked examples
Show fully solved, step-by-step examples before asking the learner to solve alone.
- Practical cases and guided analyses.
- Demonstrations before exercises.
- Contrast a poor answer vs. an acceptable one vs. an excellent one.

## 5. Formative assessment
Assessment must guide the next action, not just pass/fail.
- Feedback per topic, gap identification, review recommendation, personalized reinforcement.

## 6. Metacognition
Help the learner see what they know, what they don't, and what they only think they know.
- Confidence diagnosis, perceived-difficulty questions, gap logging, an "ignorance map", final reflection.

## 7. Bloom's taxonomy — organize objectives and assessments by cognitive level

| Cognitive level | Example objective |
|---|---|
| Remember | Define the key terms. |
| Understand | Explain in your own words. |
| Apply | Solve a typical case. |
| Analyze | Compare alternatives and find the cause of a problem. |
| Evaluate | Judge which approach is better in a scenario. |
| Create | Propose an original solution, plan, or artifact. |

Tag each learning objective and each assessment question with its Bloom level.

## Didactic rule (every lesson)
A lesson can never be passive text. Each lesson must contain **at least**:
- 1 worked example.
- 1 analogy (when useful).
- 3 active-recall questions.
- 1 mini-exercise.
- 1 "common mistakes" section.
- 1 closing summary.
